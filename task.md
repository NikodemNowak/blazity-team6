The task
Topic · AI for Content
Build a web app that uses AI to solve a real content-management pain.

The challenge
People who work with words, images, or video drown in busywork - reformatting, off-brand checking, asset hunting, keeping tone consistent. Exactly the work AI is now good at. “Content” is broad: posts, copy, docs, social, newsletters, subtitles, asset libraries. Pick a pain you understand and make it faster, clearer, or less error-prone.

The rules
Any language, framework, or stack - pick what's fastest. The one hard requirement: work inside the provided repo, pre-initialised with Atlas. Build with your AI agent - directing it, reviewing it, owning the result. The rules, decisions, and context live in the repo, not in a lost chat history.

A strong project
Targets a specific, believable content pain - not “AI that does everything.”
The AI earns its place: the tool would be worse without it.
Works end-to-end on a real example by demo time.
You can say what you built, what the AI did, and where you stepped in.
Examples
Inspiration
Ten AI content tools worth building. Pick one, remix it, or let it spark your own idea.

01
SEO & GEO Optimiser
Analyses a draft and rewrites it to rank in both search engines and AI answer engines, flagging missing keywords, structure, and citable facts.

02
Thumbnail Generator
Turns a post's title and key idea into on-brand thumbnail options with AI-generated imagery and headline text.

03
Content Recommendation Engine
Suggests what a reader (or editor) should see next by matching content against interests, gaps, and past engagement.

04
Marketing Agent
A goal-driven assistant that drafts, schedules, and adapts a small campaign's content across channels from a single brief.

05
Writing Style Extractor
Learns a writer's voice from a few samples and produces a reusable style profile new drafts can be generated or checked against.

06
Claims Research Bot
Scans a piece for factual claims and checks each one against sources, flagging the unsupported or risky ones for review.

07
Brand Voice Checker
Compares any draft to your brand guidelines and highlights where tone, terminology, or phrasing drifts off-brand.

08
Editorial Calendar Planner
Turns goals and themes into a structured publishing schedule, balancing topics, formats, and cadence across the month.

09
Post Analytics Coach
Reads a piece's performance data and returns specific, prioritised suggestions for what to improve next time.

10
One Idea, Every Format
Takes a single content idea and expands it into a tweet, a post, a newsletter, an article, a video script, and an image prompt - each tailored to its platform.

What we're judging
The whole picture
We're looking at how you think, not how much you ship.

Judges weigh the whole picture: did you target a real, specific content problem and scope it sensibly for the time? Did AI earn its place - pointed at the right work, with its output checked rather than trusted blindly? And does it actually run end-to-end on a real example by the time you demo?

Just as much, we want to see that you own the result - that you can walk us through the decisions and context behind it, and be clear about what you built, what the AI did, and where you stepped in. A thoughtful, honest, working demo beats a flashy one you can't explain.
